All About Onions

Onions are a popular root vegetable with a pungent flavor, used in various culinary dishes worldwide, from salads to cooked meals.

Description

Onions (Allium cepa) come in many varieties, including red, yellow, white, and green onions (scallions). They grow underground, developing a bulb that can be harvested and stored for long periods.

Uses of Onions

  • Commonly used in soups, stews, stir-fries, and as a seasoning in many savory dishes.
  • Fresh onions can be used in salads and sandwiches for added flavor.
  • Pickled onions are a popular side dish or condiment.
  • Onions are also used in traditional medicine to treat colds and reduce inflammation.

How to Grow Onions

  • Soil: Onions thrive in loose, well-drained soil rich in organic matter. A pH of 6.0 to 7.0 is ideal for onion growth.
  • Water: Onions need regular watering, especially during bulb formation. Keep the soil moist but not waterlogged.
  • Light: Onions prefer full sun, needing at least 6 hours of sunlight per day to grow healthy bulbs.
  • Pests and Diseases: Watch out for onion maggots, thrips, and fungal diseases like downy mildew and onion rot. Crop rotation can help reduce disease risk.

Specific Tips for Northern MN

  • Best Time to Plant: In northern Minnesota, plant onion sets or transplants in early spring, usually in late April or early May, once the soil is workable and frost danger has passed.
  • Average Growth Time: Onions take about 90 to 120 days to mature, depending on the variety.
  • Winterizing Tips: Onions are typically harvested in late summer. For overwintering onions, plant specific winter-hardy varieties in late summer or fall and cover them with mulch for insulation.
  • Storage Tips: Cure onions by letting them dry for several weeks in a warm, well-ventilated area. Once dried, store them in a cool, dry place.

Harvesting Onions

Onions are ready to harvest when the tops turn yellow and start to fall over. After pulling them from the ground, let them cure in a warm, dry, and well-ventilated area for a few weeks before storing them.

Companion Planting

Onions make excellent companion plants for carrots, lettuce, tomatoes, and cabbage. They help deter pests like aphids and beetles.
