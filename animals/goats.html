All About Goats

Goats are hardy animals known for their adaptability and usefulness in various agricultural settings.

Description

Goats (Capra aegagrus hircus) are domesticated mammals kept for their milk, meat, and fiber. They are curious and social animals that require companionship.

Uses of Goats

  • Goat milk is highly nutritious and can be used for various dairy products.
  • Goat meat, or chevon, is lean and popular in many cuisines.
  • Some breeds provide fibers, such as cashmere or mohair.
  • Goats can help clear brush and maintain pastures, making them excellent for land management.

How to Raise Goats

  • Housing: Provide a clean, dry shelter that protects goats from harsh weather and predators. Ensure good ventilation and plenty of space to roam.
  • Food: Goats are browsers and require a varied diet that includes hay, pasture, grains, and minerals. They also enjoy fruits and vegetables as treats.
  • Water: Fresh and clean water must be available at all times. Goats can drink a significant amount, especially when lactating.
  • Social Needs: Goats are social creatures and should be kept in groups to prevent loneliness and stress.

Specific Tips for Northern MN

  • Best Time to Start: Start raising goats in late spring (May) when temperatures are milder, allowing kids to acclimate before the colder months.
  • Average Growth Time: Kids typically reach market weight by 3 to 5 months, depending on the breed and purpose (meat or milk).
  • Winterizing Tips: Ensure the shelter is insulated and draft-free during winter. Provide extra bedding for warmth, and consider using heat lamps if necessary.
  • Cold-Hardy Breeds: Choose breeds that are known for their hardiness in colder climates, such as Nubian or Alpine goats.

Health and Maintenance

Regular veterinary check-ups are important. Vaccinate against common diseases, and check for parasites. Maintain a clean living environment to promote health.

Breeding

Goats typically breed in the fall, and gestation lasts about 150 days. Plan for kidding in the spring when conditions are more favorable for newborns.

Milk Production

High-producing dairy breeds can produce 1 to 2 gallons of milk per day. Regular milking and proper nutrition will ensure good milk production.
