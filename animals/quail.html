All About Quail

Quail are small, versatile birds that are easy to raise and provide delicious eggs and meat.

Description

Quail are small game birds belonging to the family Phasianidae. They are known for their distinctive calls, small size, and ability to adapt to various environments.

Uses of Quail

  • Quail eggs are a delicacy and can be used in a variety of dishes.
  • Quail meat is tender and flavorful, making it a gourmet choice for many recipes.
  • Quail can be raised for hunting and sport.
  • They can be kept as pets or for educational purposes.

How to Raise Quail

  • Housing: Quail require a clean, secure enclosure that protects them from predators and harsh weather. Ensure adequate ventilation and space for movement.
  • Food: Feed quail a high-protein diet specifically formulated for game birds, along with grains and greens.
  • Water: Provide fresh, clean water in shallow dishes or waterers to prevent drowning.
  • Breeding: Quail can be bred in small spaces. Males should be kept with multiple females to reduce aggression.

Specific Tips for Northern MN

  • Best Time to Start: Start raising quail indoors in early spring (April) when temperatures begin to warm up. This allows them to acclimate before being moved outside.
  • Average Growth Time: Quail typically reach maturity in about 6 to 8 weeks, depending on the breed.
  • Winterizing Tips: Ensure the enclosure is insulated and draft-free during winter. Use heat lamps if necessary to maintain warmth. Providing bedding like straw or shavings can help insulate the ground.
  • Cold-Hardy Breeds: Choose breeds that can withstand colder temperatures, such as Coturnix quail, which are known for their hardiness.

Health and Maintenance

Regularly check for signs of illness and keep their environment clean to prevent disease. Provide ample space for movement to reduce stress.

Egg Production

Quail hens typically start laying eggs at about 6 to 8 weeks of age. They can lay between 200 to 300 eggs per year.

Storage Tips for Eggs

  • Fresh Eggs: Store quail eggs in a cool, dry place, preferably in the refrigerator, where they can last for several weeks.
  • Preserving Eggs: Consider pickling or freezing quail eggs for longer storage options.
